4 Senses Massage and Bodywork Policies and Procedures



  • 4 Senses Massage offers full body Swedish, chair, integrative stretching, pregnancy, and deep tissue massage. Other modalities are available upon request. All these can aid in increasing circulation, detoxifying waste material from muscles, improving muscle tone, flexibility, and range of motion, decrease symptoms of pain, depression, anxiety and impart a feeling of well-being. Any and all of these therapies can also cause increased tenderness and even pain to affected areas. An increase in circulation can be harmful to some clients with certain fluid imbalances such as severe edema, CHF, high blood pressure and kidney problems. Please alert your therapist if you have any of these or any other serious conditions.

  • Sessions are scheduled by appointment only. If a client is greater than 15 minutes late to an appointment, they WILL be asked to reschedule. If a client habitually misses or arrives late for their appointments, the therapist may refuse to schedule that client again. The length of sessions are to be determined by the client and therapist at the time the appointment is made and no extra time will be afforded. All information shared in preparation for, during, and immediately after a session shall be kept confidential unless subpoenaed by a court of law or in an emergency situation.

  • Fees for services are; full body Swedish $45, deep tissue $45, chair $15, hands and feet only $30, integrative stretching $45, and hot stone $65. Cash and checks are accepted. If 4 Senses receives a returned check, the therapist will only accept cash from that client from that point on.

  • At your appointment, you will complete the intake form and discuss the session with your therapist. The therapist will then instruct you on how to prepare for the massage and will leave the room while you disrobe. Music, aroma, and reduced lighting are part of the massage experience but any client can request to eliminate any of those things to be changed to make the session more comfortable. You may be encouraged to continue stretches learned in your sessions but consult your doctor before doing any strenuous exercise or if you have pain or numbness as this may be a sign of a serious health issue.

Styles Of Massage offered



Swedish - the most commonly used form of classical Western massage, generally performed in the direction of the heart, sometimes with active or passive movement of the joints. It is used especially for relaxation, relief of muscular tension, and improvement of circulation and range of motion.


Deep tissue - A type of massage in which the fingers, thumbs and elbows are used to release chronic muscle tension, using slow, deep strokes and friction and is designed to access multiple layers of muscle and fascia to improve alignment, reduce levels of resting tension, and create more efficient postural and movement patterns.


Prenatal - It aims to relax tense muscles, ease sore spots, improve circulation and mobility, and just make you feel good. But prenatal massage is also customized to the needs of pregnant women and their changing bodies.


Chair - Chair massage is a style of seated massage that is typically short -- 10 or 15 minutes -- and focuses on your back, shoulders and neck and arms. Chair massage is done over clothes and doesn't require any massage oil.


Hands and Feet - A 30 minute massage focusing only on the forearms and hands, and lower legs and feet only. Helps with carpal tunnel and, wrist and ankle mobility.


Hot Stone - Hot stone massage is a specialty massage where the therapist uses smooth, heated stones as an extension of their own hands, or by placing them on the body. The heat can be both deeply relaxing and help warm up tight muscles so the therapist can work more deeply, more quickly. 


Integrated Stretching - a full body stretch, where the client is fully clothed and no oil is used. The therapist uses stretching techniques that will help with stiffness, mobility, range of motion, and circulation.
